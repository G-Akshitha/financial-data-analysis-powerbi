# 📊 Financial Data Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing financial data of multiple companies using **Power BI dashboards**. The goal is to explore financial performance, identify trends, and compare companies using key financial metrics such as **Revenue, Net Income, Assets, Liabilities, Cash Flow, and Profitability ratios**.

The analysis is divided into multiple dashboards to provide insights from different financial perspectives including **Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, and KPI comparisons**.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze **financial performance of companies over time**
* Compare **revenue, profit, and asset growth across companies**
* Evaluate **cash flow trends and liquidity**
* Identify companies with **strong profitability and financial stability**
* Understand **year-over-year financial changes**

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies

* **Power BI** – Data visualization and dashboard creation
* **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Calculations and measures
* **Financial Dataset** – Company financial data including revenue, assets, liabilities, and cash flows

---

---

## 📈 Key Financial Metrics Explained

**Revenue**
Total income generated from business operations.

**Net Income**
Profit remaining after deducting all expenses.

**Gross Profit Margin**
Percentage of revenue remaining after deducting cost of revenue.

**Operating Cash Flow**
Cash generated from core business operations.

**Free Cash Flow**
Cash available after operating expenses and capital expenditures.

**Debt-to-Equity Ratio**
Measures the proportion of debt used compared to shareholder equity.

---

## 🔍 Insights from the Analysis

* Some companies demonstrate **consistent revenue growth across years**
* **Net income fluctuates** due to varying operational expenses
* Several companies show **high operating cash flows**, indicating strong core business performance
* Financial institutions typically have **higher liabilities due to large-scale operations**
* Some companies show **negative free cash flow due to heavy investments**
